Asthma is a chronic respiratory disease that causes inflammation and narrowing of the airways, making breathing difficult.

Symptoms:
- Shortness of breath
- Wheezing
- Chest tightness
- Persistent coughing, especially at night or early morning

Common Triggers:
- Dust mites
- Pollen
- Pet dander
- Cold air
- Exercise
- Respiratory infections
- Smoke and air pollution

Diagnosis:
Doctors diagnose asthma through medical history, physical examination, spirometry, peak flow measurement, and allergy testing.

Treatment:
Asthma is commonly treated with inhaled corticosteroids, bronchodilators (rescue inhalers), long-term controller medications, and avoiding known triggers.

Prevention:
Avoid allergens and irritants, follow the prescribed treatment plan, carry a rescue inhaler, monitor symptoms regularly, and attend routine medical checkups.
