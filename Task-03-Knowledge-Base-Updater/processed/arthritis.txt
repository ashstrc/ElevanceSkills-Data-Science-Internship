Arthritis is a condition that causes inflammation and stiffness in one or more joints. It is one of the most common causes of chronic pain and disability, especially in older adults.

Symptoms:
- Joint pain
- Swelling
- Stiffness, especially in the morning
- Reduced range of motion
- Redness around the affected joint

Common Types:
- Osteoarthritis
- Rheumatoid Arthritis
- Psoriatic Arthritis
- Gout

Risk Factors:
- Aging
- Family history
- Obesity
- Previous joint injuries
- Autoimmune diseases

Diagnosis:
Doctors diagnose arthritis through physical examination, medical history, blood tests, X-rays, MRI scans, and joint fluid analysis.

Treatment:
Treatment depends on the type and severity of arthritis. Common treatments include pain relievers, anti-inflammatory medications (NSAIDs), corticosteroids, disease-modifying antirheumatic drugs (DMARDs), physical therapy, regular exercise, weight management, and in severe cases, joint replacement surgery.

Prevention:
Maintain a healthy weight, stay physically active, protect your joints from injury, eat a balanced diet, and seek medical advice if joint pain persists.
