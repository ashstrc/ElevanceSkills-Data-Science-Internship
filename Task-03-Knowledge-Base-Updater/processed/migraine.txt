Migraine is a neurological condition characterized by recurring headaches.

Symptoms:
- Severe throbbing headache
- Nausea
- Vomiting
- Sensitivity to light
- Sensitivity to sound

Risk Factors:
- Family history
- Hormonal changes
- Stress
- Lack of sleep

Diagnosis:
Doctors diagnose migraines based on symptoms, medical history, and neurological examinations.

Treatment:
Treatment may include pain relievers, triptans, preventive medications, hydration, and adequate rest.

Prevention:
Maintain regular sleep patterns, avoid known triggers, stay hydrated, and manage stress effectively.
